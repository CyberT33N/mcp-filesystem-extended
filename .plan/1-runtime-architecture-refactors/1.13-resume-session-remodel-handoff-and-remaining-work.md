## Already 100% Complete — Must Not Be Re-Decided
[INTENT: REFERENZ]

### 1. Architectural decisions that are closed

The following architectural surfaces are already closed and must **not** be re-decided:

1. **Public request token vocabulary**
   - The target-state public request field is `resumeToken`.
   - `continuationToken` is not the target-state public vocabulary anymore.

2. **Public response envelope vocabulary**
   - The authoritative response object is `resume`.
   - The older public `continuation` object is not the target-state contract anymore.

3. **Resume intent modeling**
   - Preview-capable families support `resumeMode = 'next-chunk'` and `resumeMode = 'complete-result'`.
   - `count_lines` supports only `resumeMode = 'complete-result'`.

4. **Admission vocabulary**
   - The target-state public lane is `completion-backed-required`.
   - The old public wording `task-backed-required` is not the final public contract.

5. **Scope reduction rule**
   - Scope reduction remains a first-class alternative to resume.
   - Endpoint and server guidance must explicitly surface narrowing guidance instead of treating resume as the only next step.

6. **Same-endpoint resume rule**
   - Resume remains same-endpoint.
   - No second public resume endpoint may be introduced.
   - No query resend may be introduced for resume-only requests.

7. **Server-owned completion rule**
   - `complete-result` means a server-owned completion attempt.
   - It is **not** a hard-cap bypass.

8. **`count_lines` specialization rule**
   - `count_lines` remains completion-backed only.
   - It must never regress into preview-style partial totals.

9. **Family-wide frontier hardening rule**
   - Preview-family frontier advancement must be commit-based.
   - No family is allowed to rely on pre-advance semantics as the final target state.
